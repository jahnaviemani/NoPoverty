Universal Credit - UK

Universal Credit is a social security payment in the UK designed to support low-income individuals and families.

It replaces six previous benefits, including Jobseeker’s Allowance and Housing Benefit.

Eligible claimants receive a basic allowance, with extra payments for children, housing, and disabilities.

Monthly payments range from £292 to £578, depending on household circumstances.

Encourages recipients to seek employment while maintaining financial support.

Over 5.8 million people in the UK currently receive Universal Credit.

The program aims to reduce poverty and simplify the benefits system.
